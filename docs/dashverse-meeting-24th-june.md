# Dashverse Meeting : 24th June  

# **Dashverse — Founder Conversation Notes**

**Counterpart:** Sanidhya (Founder, Dashverse) **Purpose:** Understand Dashverse's needs and stance on a potential Wiom TV partnership

## **1. Their Core Problem: Supply, Not Distribution**

  - **Distribution is solved.** They acquire users easily and lock them into autopay.
  - **Retention is the real challenge.** Once users are in, keeping them engaged is hard.
  - **Founder's diagnosis:** users expect *infinite* content, like Instagram or YouTube — and feeding that appetite is the bottleneck.

## **2. Content Economics — Where They're Struggling**

  - **Imported content is expensive.** To chase the "infinite content" goal, they keep buying from Chinese and Korean distributors — costs are high and they appear to be struggling with this financially.
  - **AI content is a vision, not a reality.** They want to produce AI-generated content, but cost and viability are unproven. Sounds aspirational at this stage.
  - **Local content is thin (\~10%).** They believe Indian audiences need India-specific content and see a **large supply gap** in this market.

## **3. Their \#1 Ask Right Now**

Whoever they partner with should either:

  - **Bridge their supply gap**, or
  - **Subsidise their supply**

## **4. What They Will NOT Do**

  - **No free content.** Sanidhya was explicit — they will not give content to use for free.
  - **No tracking tech.** They will not invest in any technology that enables tracking.
  - **No variable / flexible pricing.** Pricing is fixed; reselling only.
  - **They buy content from resellers themselves.** They sit on the buying side too, so they expect to be paid as a reseller — not to give content away.

## **5. What They ARE Open To**

  - **Reselling content for a paid trial.** Pitched **100 microdramas at \~$1,000–1,200 each** to buy and try → **\~₹1 crore in revenue** for them, which solves their supply-subsidising problem.
  - **Sending users to their app.** They're somewhat convinced on the **incremental TG** our platform offers, and view the **TAM as large** (\~14 players each taking a share). They proposed launching something that **directs our users to their app.**
  - **Sharing content learnings for the pilot** — but only **if the users are on their app.**
  - **Extended free coupons** — which they don't usually offer to others, again if users are directed to their app.

## **6. The Deal Shape — Two Paths Only**

Dashverse's position reduces to a binary:

|  |  |  |
| :-: | :-: | :-: |
| \*\*Option\*\* | \*\*What it means\*\* | \*\*What it solves for them\*\* |
| \*\*A. Buy content from them\*\* | Resell content on trial — paid, fixed price, no free, no variable pricing | Subsidises their supply |
| \*\*B. Send users to their app\*\* | Drive our incremental user base to Dashverse; they share content learnings as long as users sit on their app | Adds incremental TG / revenue |

**There is no third way.** Either buy their content, or send them your users.

## **Net Takeaway**

Dashverse is open to **reselling content for a trial** (nothing free, no variable pricing) and to **tapping our incremental user base** by having us route users to their app. Both routes are framed entirely around solving *their* supply and revenue problem — not around investing in our platform or measurement.

## **Net Learnings**

  - **A platform business like Dashverse is worried about its whole LTV P\&L.** What they care about is anything that adds the right subsidy at the right side of the see-saw — not the feature or the partner per se.
  - **For a platform that buys its content and monetises via subscriptions, tapping into distribution only makes sense if it can pull users onto its own app.** Distribution is attractive to them strictly as a funnel into their own subscription economics.
