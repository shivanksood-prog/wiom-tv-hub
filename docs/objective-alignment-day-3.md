From Shivank · Wiom TV · Post-meeting alignment note · Day 3

*This note captures my thinking after the Day 3 meeting. It is mostly what we discussed, written down for my own clarity — the aligned objective, the clarification that reshapes who we build for, and the resulting Phase 1 definition.*

# **Aligned objective**

Although the broader goal of Wiom TV is a separate business line — connecting with users where our pipes do not physically reach — it also helps Wiom become a distribution platform: a surface on which we can eventually sell multiple services and/or content, and a way to build a relationship with the “dreamers” we are not connected with today.

# **Clarifications**

Is the objective also to sell these people “Recharge-wala Ghar ka Net” (RWGKN)? **Yes.**

But that is not the main goal. As discussed, it could be other things too. This is a separate business line that can have a benefit to the current internet business — but that benefit is not the primary objective.

This clarification is what determines our direction: who we are targeting, and who we are building for.

### **The ideal persona**

With this clarification, the ideal persona is no longer Pre-WiFi households only — which is exactly what the clarification changes. A clearer way to see it is a 2×2:

|  |  |  |
| :-: | :-: | :-: |
|   | \*\*Connected TV\*\* | \*\*No Connected TV\*\* |
| \*\*Post-WiFi\*\* | Has WiFi + Smart TV (our Phase 1 base) | Has WiFi, no connected TV |
| \*\*Pre-WiFi\*\* | Cable/DTH TV, no WiFi | Mobile-only, no WiFi, no TV |

  

Our objective values a Pre-WiFi / No-TV person equally to a Post-WiFi / Connected-TV person. We are not optimising for the pre-WiFi household alone.

# **Phase 1**

Keeping this larger objective in mind, we define the objective of Phase 1.

  - **No acquisition spend.** In Phase 1 we do not spend money to get new users.
  - **Build on current learnings.** To avoid the time cost of reinventing an app from scratch, we build on what already exists.
  - **We already have a subset of the target persona.** It is within our base, which also helps us make the offering smooth.

The distilled objective is not to make Wiom TV a “bundled” offering for Wiom WiFi users. Here “bundled” does not mean linked or gated — it implies merely sweetening the deal for someone already on Wiom. Instead, the objective is to make Wiom TV the primary entertainment for these users. There is no gating with our plans.

**Objective:**  Make Wiom TV the default entertainment option for Wiom RWGKN users.

**Persona:**  Wiom-at-home, cord-cutters — wants to, or has, cut Cable TV; Smart TV owner.

**Surface:**  The connected / Smart-TV surface (the existing Wiom TV app) is best for the Phase 1 persona and audience. We will consider building a separate mobile app, or other surfaces, if customer need points us there.

# **View on legalisation**

Amber legalisation is definitely not sustainable in the longer term. But it is the perfect grey area we need right now — for quick insight-gathering and for keeping momentum as we move forward.

# **Caution — how we interpret data in zero-to-one**

When consuming already-formed data, I need to be careful not to assume the data is pointing toward an insight, or toward the absence of a product.

Example: “86% use mobile as their primary device.” Is that pointing toward mobile being the right platform — or toward the absence of something the customer needs? We need to chase what the customer needs, not what the surface of the data appears to say.
