Reading notes

  1. Arm B is the story so far: leads activation (+59% rel.), D1 (2.1× control), median watch time, and dwell-per-switch — users hunt less and land on keepers, consistent with the reorder doing its job. None of it is significant yet.
  2. Arm A trends negative everywhere including D1 (3.4% vs 6.5%). Part of the dwell deficit is mechanical (the intro eats first-session seconds), but that does not explain weaker day-1 return. If this persists at ~2× sample, consider stopping A for harm while B runs to power.
  3. Watch-time means are untrustworthy at this n: one control user watched 192 min (arm sd=17). Use medians. The raw live dashboard's 9.6 min/user for B is one 19.6 h broken-era carryover user — excluded here; dashboard fix pending.
  4. Multiplicity: two treatment-vs-control comparisons at α=0.05 each, per convention set before readout.
  5. Lost era Jul 3–6 (server never reloaded): 1,109 enrolled users unattributable — the reason this is underpowered despite 5 days on the calendar.
