1 Distribute on a newer screen TV · stream hosted by the partner›

Stream delivery

  • HLS (TS or fMP4), CDN-fronted, 24×7 origin with a stated uptime commitment. The partner hosts — Wiom pulls and showcases the channel, it does not take delivery of the feed.
  • At least three ABR renditions — 1080p / 720p / 480p. Our floor is a cheap Android TV box on a shared connection, so the low rung matters more than the top one.
  • H.264 + AAC-LC. HEVC-only feeds will not decode reliably on the boxes we actually reach.
  • No IP allowlisting, or an allowlist that accepts our egress range. Signed URLs are fine if the TTL clears a full sachet.
  • Notice period before any origin or URL change.

Rights & protection

  • Written confirmation of India distribution rights for CTV / connected-TV apps.
  • Clear HLS preferred for v1. If DRM is mandatory: Widevine L3, licence URL and token spec up front — it changes our build cost materially.
  • Geo-fencing and blackout windows, if any, with the mechanism to enforce them.

Metadata & EPG

  • Now / next at minimum; 24h forward schedule preferred. XMLTV or JSON.
  • Channel logo — transparent PNG or SVG, 512px or better.
  • Channel name in Hindi and English, plus genre and audio language.

Reporting

  • Wiom reports monthly: minutes watched and sachet units per channel. No entitlement API needed — the sachet unlock happens in the Wiom TV app, so the gate is ours even though the stream is yours.
2 Sell on the Sachet Store Mobile · Wiom TV mobile surface›

Catalogue feed

  • Machine-readable catalogue (JSON or CSV): title, episode, duration, language, maturity rating, availability window.
  • Artwork in both ratios — 2:3 portrait and 16:9 landscape — because the store and the player need different crops.
  • Refresh cadence agreed up front: full replace or delta, and how fast a takedown propagates.

Playback

  • A content stream for on-demand viewing — per-title HLS or MP4, mobile-web playable, same codec floor as offer 1.
  • If content stays on the partner origin: a signed-URL or token endpoint whose TTL outlives the sachet the customer bought.

Commercial plumbing

  • Wiom is merchant of record. The partner needs no billing integration at all.
  • Title-level reporting monthly: plays, sachet units, revenue statement, with a stated dispute window.
3 Sell into your own platform Mobile · on the partner's platform›
→
This is the only offer with real two-way integration. Everything below exists because money moves on events the partner controls, so the events have to be defined before the rates are.

Handoff

  • Seamless authenticated handoff — a deep link from Wiom into the partner platform carrying a Wiom-signed token, so the user arrives already recognised and never logs in twice.
  • Unlock API — a sachet bought on Wiom must open the content on the partner platform without a second payment or a second login, and repeat sachet purchase must keep working.
  • Graceful no-app path: what the link does when the partner app is not installed.

Attribution — this is what the install revenue is paid on

  • One written definition of "install": store install, first open, or first authenticated session. The three give very different numbers.
  • Install Referrer or an agreed attribution SDK, plus the attribution window in days.
  • Server-to-server postback for purchases made on the partner platform by Wiom-referred users — signed payload, retry policy, idempotency key.
  • Fraud controls and an agreed ceiling — standard on any per-install commercial, and it protects the payout on both sides.

Reconciliation

  • An explicit dedupe rule. Customers may keep buying sachets on the Wiom TV mobile surface after installing the partner platform, so the same user can otherwise be claimed on both sides.
  • Shared monthly file, matching keys agreed in advance, dispute window stated.

Before signature

  • Sandbox credentials and test content — we will not integrate against production first.
  • A named technical contact and a response-time expectation for stream-down incidents.
  • Brand assets and usage guidelines, so the store and the guide can render the partner correctly.
  • Data and PII posture: what we log, what we pass on, what neither side may retain.

Open on our side — resolve before a partner asks

  • Play billing policy. Whether in-app digital content on Android can be sold over UPI at all, or must route through Google Play, changes every revenue-share number downstream.
  • UPI wait, 20–40 seconds. Every prototype has to cover it without stopping the picture — a product constraint that becomes a partner-facing quality claim.
