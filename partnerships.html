What follows is written from the pipeline that actually runs today: the live channel config, the web health probe, the stream validator and the microdrama delivery build. Every line is tagged Must (we cannot ship a partner without it) or v2 (welcome, never a blocker). This is now the answer we give partners; changes go through an explicit revision, not an edit.

What our surfaces can and cannot carry — as built today

Five facts that decide most conversations. Stating them first stops a deal from dying in week six over something that was true in week one.

✓
Clear HLS, both surfacesTS or fMP4 over HTTPS. H.264 (Main) + AAC-LC.
✕
No DRM, of any kindWidevine, PlayReady or SAMPLE-AES feeds are rejected by our validator and will not play. This is a disqualifier, not a cost line.
✕
No DASH / MPDThe TV app builds an HLS source only.
✓
CORS-open originsThe web surface health-probes every playlist from the browser. A feed that refuses the probe is treated as dead.
✓
Live in five minutesA channel is one row in a hosted config we poll every 5 minutes. The same path pulls it down just as fast.
✓
A ~1.5 Mbps floorOur reference device is a cheap Android TV box on a shared connection. The low rung matters more than the top one.

Stream delivery

Onboarding is one config row: name, logo URL, genre, language, stream URL. You host and deliver; Wiom pulls and showcases the channel. There is no feed hand-over and no integration to build.

  • MustClear HLS over HTTPS — master or media playlist, HTTP 200 to a standard browser or TV user-agent.
  • MustA stable URL. No per-session token, no expiring query string, no Referer or User-Agent allowlist — a browser cannot set those, so such a feed plays in the TV app and dies on the web surface.
  • MustCORS open to our origins on the playlist and the segments, with HEAD allowed. Our web client probes every channel from the browser; a channel that fails the probe is marked dead and skipped, however well it plays elsewhere.
  • MustAt least one rendition under ~1.5 Mbps, H.264 Main + AAC-LC. HEVC-only feeds will not decode reliably on the boxes we actually reach.
  • MustSegments ≤10 s and a live window of at least three segments. Our player buffers 8–15 s and abandons a playlist at 6 s.
  • MustPlaylist responds in under 2 s. The health probe aborts at 5 s.
  • MustNotice before any origin or URL change. Silently moved URLs are the single largest source of channel loss on the platform.
  • v2A full ABR ladder — 1080p / 720p / 480p. Welcome, not required: a large part of the current line-up is single-rendition and ships fine.

Rights & protection

  • MustWritten confirmation of India distribution rights for connected-TV / OTT redistribution. This is the highest-value line on the page — it is what moves a channel out of the unverified bucket.
  • MustThe stream must be clear. Repeating it here because it is usually raised late: we carry no DRM on either surface today.
  • v2Geo-fencing and blackout windows. We have no enforcement mechanism today, so if your rights require one, raise it before signature — it is a build, not a setting.

What we need to list the channel

Only what the config row actually holds. Anything richer we would file and not render.

  • MustChannel name in English and Hindi, plus genre and audio language.
  • MustLogo as a hosted URL — transparent PNG, 512px or better. We reference the URL; we do not take a file.
  • v2EPG — now/next or a 24h schedule, XMLTV or JSON. There is no guide surface to render it in yet. Send it when we build one.

Operations & reporting

  • MustA named technical contact and a response-time expectation for a dead stream.
  • Wiom reports monthly: minutes watched and sachet units per channel. No entitlement API needed — the sachet unlock happens in the Wiom TV app, so the gate is ours even though the stream is yours.
  • Takedown is ours and it is fast: we can put a channel live, or pull it, within five minutes of the ask.

You write no code for this one. We encode, host, deliver, sell and report. What we need from you is files and rights, not an API — which is exactly what content partners can hand over on day one.

What you send us — once

  • MustMezzanine masters — H.264 MP4 at the best quality you hold, vertical 480×854 or better (or 720p landscape). A Drive, S3 or FTP link is fine; we pull once and take it from there.
  • MustEpisode boundaries and durations — a CSV is enough — if you are sending full-length masters for us to cut.
  • MustOne metadata row per title: title, episode count, language, maturity rating, rights window, and a 2:3 poster. Landscape art is welcome, not required.
  • MustClean masters for anything monetised — no third-party watermark. Watermarked cuts are fine for a pilot; they cannot carry a paid sachet.
  • v2A machine-readable catalogue feed with delta refresh and takedown propagation. Worth building only once the library is large and fast-moving.

What we do — so it is not asked of you

  • Encoding, hosting and delivery are ours. Per-episode progressive MP4 on our CDN. No partner origin, no signed-URL endpoint, no token service on your side.
  • Wiom is merchant of record. No billing integration at all.
  • Versioned, never overwritten. A re-cut ships as a new version; a file already live is never replaced in place.
  • No DRM on this surface either — same constraint as the TV.

Commercial plumbing

  • MustA takedown SLA in writing. We can pull a title within hours; we need to know what you require of us.
  • Title-level reporting monthly from us: plays, minutes, sachet units, revenue statement, with a stated dispute window.

This is the only offer with real two-way integration. Everything below exists because money moves on events you control, so the events have to be defined before the rates are.

Entitlement & handoff

  • MustDefine what a Wiom sachet entitles on your platform — how long, and across what: a window, a title, a season. This is the first unanswered question in the offer, and every rate downstream depends on the answer.
  • MustA redemption path, voucher first. v1: a code we mint and your app redeems — you almost certainly already run this machinery for trial pricing. It ships in days and does not require you to accept our identity system.
  • v2Deep link with a Wiom-signed token, so the user arrives already recognised and never logs in twice. The better experience, the much larger build — sequence it second.
  • MustA graceful no-app path — what the link does when your app is not installed.
  • MustA price-ladder rule. Ours is a ₹10 sachet; yours is a monthly pack. Agree in writing when a Wiom-referred customer may be upsold, because that upsell is the exact event both rate lines are paid on.

Attribution — this is what the install revenue is paid on

  • MustOne written definition of "install": store install, first open, or first authenticated session. The three give very different numbers.
  • MustInstall Referrer or an agreed attribution SDK, plus the attribution window in days.
  • MustA shared matching key — hashed mobile number. Both platforms already identify a household by phone; it is the only key that survives a reinstall and the only one that makes dedupe enforceable.
  • MustServer-to-server postbacks on a defined event list, agreed before rates: install → first open → first paid → subscription or autopay start → refund, chargeback or cancellation. Signed payload, retry policy, idempotency key.
  • MustFraud controls and an agreed ceiling — standard on any per-install commercial, and it protects the payout on both sides.

Reconciliation

  • MustAn explicit dedupe rule. Customers may keep buying sachets on the Wiom TV mobile surface after installing your platform, so the same user can otherwise be claimed on both sides. Hashed-phone matching is the mechanism.
  • MustReversal handling. What happens to a payout already made when the purchase behind it is refunded or charged back. Absent this, both sides pay out on revenue neither side keeps.
  • Shared monthly file, matching keys agreed in advance, dispute window stated.

Before signature

  • MustSandbox credentials and test content — we will not integrate against production first.
  • MustA named technical contact and a response-time expectation for stream-down incidents.
  • MustBrand assets and usage guidelines, so the store and the guide can render you correctly.
  • MustData and PII posture: what we log, what we pass on, what neither side may retain.

Open on our side — resolve before a partner asks

  • Play billing policy. Whether in-app digital content on Android can be sold over UPI at all, or must route through Google Play, changes every revenue-share number downstream.
  • UPI wait, 20–40 seconds. Every prototype has to cover it without stopping the picture — a product constraint that becomes a partner-facing quality claim.
  • DRM. We carry none today, on either surface. If a partner worth having requires it, that is a build decision to take deliberately — not something to discover mid-negotiation.
  • No guide surface yet. Until one exists we cannot render an EPG, so we should stop asking for schedules we would only file.
